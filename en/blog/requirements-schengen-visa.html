Updated requirements for the Schengen visa

Complete guide to documentation, interview and timelines for Latin American applicants.

The short-stay Schengen visa (type C) allows entry into 29 European countries for up to 90 days within a 180-day window. Although the regulatory framework is shared, each Member State may impose additional requirements and retains the power to approve or reject applications.

Basic documentation

  • Passport with validity of at least 3 months past the return date and at least 2 blank pages.
  • Completed and signed application form.
  • Recent biometric photographs (35x45 mm, white background).
  • Round-trip flight reservation (paid ticket not required).
  • Accommodation reservation or invitation letter.
  • Proof of financial solvency (bank statements from the last 3–6 months).
  • Travel medical insurance with minimum coverage of EUR 30,000.
  • Employment letter or enrollment certificate.

Before the interview

The consular interview is the decisive moment. Some preparation recommendations:

  1. Know your itinerary and purpose of travel in depth.
  2. Be clear about your ties to your home country (employment, family, property).
  3. Do not contradict the documentation submitted.
  4. Bring documents organized, ideally in original and copy.
No requirement replaces a coherent presentation. Documentation demonstrates facts; the interview demonstrates coherence between the facts and the applicant.

Response times

Timelines vary by consulate and season:

  • Statutory period: up to 15 calendar days.
  • During high season (Northern summer): may extend to 30–45 days.
  • It is recommended to start the procedure at least 60 days in advance.

Common grounds for refusal

Identifying the most common refusal reasons helps prevent them:

  • Insufficient or unverified financial solvency.
  • Undefined or inconsistent itinerary.
  • Weak ties to the country of origin.
  • Formal errors in the form or documentation.
  • Applying to the wrong consulate.

Professional advisory

Every case is unique. A prior evaluation with a specialized attorney allows you to identify weaknesses before submitting the application and plan for an appeal in case of refusal.
