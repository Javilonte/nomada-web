FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Clear answers to the most common questions. If you cannot find yours, please contact us.

It depends on the visa type and the jurisdiction. A tourist visa may be resolved in 10–20 business days; a work or residence visa can take 8 to 16 weeks; permanent residence or family reunification between 6 and 12 months. These timelines are indicative and are confirmed during the initial consultation.

Our fees vary according to the complexity of the case. We work with a fixed quote delivered in writing after the first consultation. Consular fees and official charges are paid separately, directly to the consulate, or itemised in the quote.

No. No serious law firm can guarantee the outcome of a visa application, since the final decision rests with the consular or immigration authority. What we do guarantee is rigorous preparation, complete documentation and a defensive strategy in case of a refusal.

Yes. We serve clients in over 25 countries. Most procedures can be handled remotely through notarized powers of attorney and secure digital communication. For cases requiring physical presence we coordinate appointments at itinerant consulates or diplomatic representations.

For the first consultation, your valid ID is enough. If you already have a passport, background checks, or other case-related documents, please bring them along. We will then indicate exactly which documentation to bring to formally begin the procedure.

Yes. Our entire team works bilingually in English and Spanish. Service, document drafting and consular communications can be carried out in either language depending on the jurisdiction of the procedure.

We analyze the official refusal notice, identify the reason and evaluate the available remedies: a new application, reconsideration or, depending on the country, an administrative or judicial appeal. We advise you on deadlines and probabilities before any decision is made.

Standard renewal takes between 2 and 5 weeks depending on the country. If the passport was lost or stolen, the process may extend to 3–8 weeks due to additional documentation. For emergencies, we handle urgent procedures that can be resolved in 24–72 hours depending on the consulate.

Yes. We handle the Hague Apostille and other international legalization procedures, including certified translations, to ensure the document is valid in the destination country.

We accept bank transfer, credit/debit card and, in certain cases, international payment platforms. The first consultation can be paid at the end of the meeting; regular procedures are split into payments aligned with case milestones.
